# Legal Notes

These notes describe intended product boundaries for the Secondhand Trust Link MVP. They are not legal advice.

## Product position

Secondhand Trust Link is a non-custodial, static information and coordination layer for secondhand transactions. The project can help present transaction context and safety reminders, but it does not intermediate payment, asset transfer, custody, or dispute resolution.

## Explicit non-goals

The MVP does not provide:

- Escrow, custody, settlement, clearing, or payment processing.
- Crypto exchange, token swap, fiat on-ramp, or fiat off-ramp services.
- Hosted wallet, platform wallet, private-key management, or wallet recovery.
- Buyer protection, seller protection, insurance, chargeback handling, arbitration, or legal enforcement.
- Legal, tax, investment, compliance, or financial advice.

## Suggested user-facing disclaimer

Secondhand Trust Link does not hold money or digital assets. It does not exchange crypto or fiat currency and does not provide a platform wallet. Buyers and sellers remain responsible for verifying each other, selecting their own payment method, and deciding whether to complete a transaction.

## Review checklist before launch

- Confirm all UI copy avoids words such as escrow, protected payment, guaranteed, insured, settlement, wallet, exchange, swap, deposit, or withdrawal unless reviewed by counsel.
- Confirm no forms collect private keys, seed phrases, account credentials, card data, bank data, or government identity documents.
- Confirm no third-party script creates payment, wallet, exchange, analytics, or tracking obligations beyond the stated MVP scope.
- Confirm the GitHub Pages site does not imply affiliation with marketplaces, payment providers, wallet vendors, or regulators.
- Confirm open-source license choice and trademark naming before public promotion.

## Open-source policy suggestion

Use public development to build trust around safety copy and deployment transparency, but keep the license decision explicit. If the project wants broad reuse, prefer MIT or Apache-2.0 after legal review. If the project wants public inspection without broad reuse yet, publish without an OSI license temporarily and document that reuse rights are not granted until a license is added.
